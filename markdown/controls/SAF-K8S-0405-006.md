# SAF-K8S-0405-006 - Attribute-based access control for AI artifacts

- Domain: D04 - Identity, Access, and Secrets Management
- Knowledge Area: 4.5 - Identity Abuse Detection and Mitigation
- Maturity: Advanced
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Organizations must implement attribute-based access control (ABAC) for AI artifacts, enforcing access decisions based on data classification level, model sensitivity rating, and deployment environment (development, staging, production) to ensure that only authorized personnel and workloads can access artifacts matching their clearance and environment authorization.

## Objective

Extend access control beyond role-based permissions to incorporate contextual attributes of AI artifacts, ensuring that access to models trained on sensitive data, high-value proprietary models, and production-deployed artifacts is governed by the sensitivity and classification of the artifacts themselves.

## Rationale

RBAC alone cannot express access policies that depend on the properties of the accessed artifact. A data scientist authorized to work with public datasets should not automatically access models trained on PII or regulated health data. Similarly, access to production model endpoints should be restricted differently than access to development experiments. ABAC enables fine-grained policies that consider data classification, model sensitivity, and deployment context in authorization decisions, preventing unauthorized access to high-value or regulated AI artifacts.

## Scope

Applies to all AI artifacts including trained models, model checkpoints, training datasets, feature stores, and inference endpoints across all environments. Covers OPA/Gatekeeper or Kyverno policies that evaluate resource labels and annotations for classification and sensitivity attributes. Must integrate with the organization's data classification framework and deployment environment taxonomy.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Policy engine (OPA/Gatekeeper, Kyverno, or Cedar) evaluating artifact classification labels and annotations against requestor attributes, with admission policies and authorization webhooks enforcing ABAC decisions.
- Implementation Surface: Policy engines (OPA/Gatekeeper Rego policies, Kyverno ClusterPolicies, Cedar policies) evaluating resource classification labels (data-classification, model-sensitivity) and deployment environment annotations against requestor identity attributes, admission webhooks and authorization webhooks enforcing ABAC decisions, artifact labeling standards for models, datasets, and inference endpoints, integration with organizational data classification frameworks (Domain 7 classification labels), and policy decision audit logs for ABAC evaluations.
- Owner Primary: Security Engineering
- Owner Supporting: ML Engineering; Data Governance; Platform Engineering
