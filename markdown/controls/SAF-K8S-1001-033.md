# SAF-K8S-1001-033 - Centralized aggregation onboarding and export for cluster-wide AI logs

- Domain: D10 - Observability, Incident Response, and Governance
- Knowledge Area: 10.1 - Logging and Audit
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: baseline

## Statement

Organizations must aggregate collected Kubernetes and AI-related cluster logs into a centralized logging platform through approved onboarding or provider export paths so security monitoring and investigation can operate on unified log streams.

## Objective

Ensure collected Kubernetes and AI-related cluster logs are onboarded or exported into centralized aggregation platforms for monitoring and investigation.

## Rationale

Comprehensive log capture is not useful if collected logs never reach the centralized platform used by the SOC. Centralized aggregation and provider log export are independent control surfaces that determine whether captured cluster evidence becomes available for monitoring, search, and investigation.

## Scope

Applies to production and pre-production Kubernetes clusters hosting AI workloads. Covers centralized log aggregation services, provider log export integrations, and centralized platform onboarding for collected node, container, managed-service, and GPU-related logs. Excludes source-side collector deployment and coverage verification.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Centralized logging platform onboarding and provider export integration.
- Implementation Surface: Centralized aggregation platforms such as OpenSearch, Loki, Splunk, or equivalent services; provider log export integration; and onboarding of collected cluster log streams into centralized monitoring and investigation platforms.
- Owner Primary: Security Operations
- Owner Supporting: Platform Engineering; Infrastructure Engineering
