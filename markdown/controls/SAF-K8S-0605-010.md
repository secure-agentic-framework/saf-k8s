# SAF-K8S-0605-010 - AI custom resource webhook abuse resistance and resource hardening

- Domain: D06 - Supply Chain, Images, and Admission Control
- Knowledge Area: 6.5 - Admission Control
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Webhook endpoints that validate AI custom resources must be hardened against abuse and denial-of-service through rate limiting, resource constraints, and deployment protections that preserve validation service availability under expected and adversarial load.

## Objective

Keep AI-CRD validation services available and resistant to abuse so adversaries cannot disable or degrade policy enforcement by overwhelming webhook backends.

## Rationale

Even when validation logic is correct, weakly resourced or unprotected webhook endpoints can be exhausted by bursty or malicious request patterns, creating denial-of-service conditions and pressure to weaken enforcement. Endpoint hardening preserves operational resilience for AI admission controls.

## Scope

Applies to all validating admission webhooks dedicated to AI/ML operator CRDs in production, staging, and shared development clusters with GPU node pools.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Webhook backend deployment and serving infrastructure for AI CRD validation.
- Implementation Surface: Webhook backend rate limits, container resource requests and limits, concurrency controls, and deployment hardening for KServe, Kubeflow Training Operator, KubeRay, Volcano, and similar AI operator admission services.
- Owner Primary: Platform Engineering
- Owner Supporting: ML Platform Engineering; Security Engineering
