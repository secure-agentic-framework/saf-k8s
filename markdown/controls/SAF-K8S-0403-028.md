# SAF-K8S-0403-028 - Exposed credential revocation and replacement execution

- Domain: D04 - Identity, Access, and Secrets Management
- Knowledge Area: 4.3 - Secrets Management
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: baseline

## Statement

Organizations must revoke and replace exposed credentials on defined timelines when secret leaks are detected so compromised secrets do not remain usable after discovery.

## Objective

Ensure exposed credentials are actually revoked and reissued promptly after a leak is detected.

## Rationale

Revocation and replacement are independently auditable from the broader incident workflow. This control isolates the credential-lifecycle action so teams can verify that exposed secrets, tokens, and keys are invalidated and replaced within defined response windows.

## Scope

Applies to any detected exposure of credentials, tokens, keys, or secrets in repositories, images, CI/CD artifacts, logs, and runtime outputs. Covers credential revocation timing, replacement or reissuance, and evidence that exposed secrets no longer remain valid. Incident triage and containment remain governed by SAF-K8S-0403-027.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Credential revocation and reissuance process triggered by secret leak findings.
- Implementation Surface: Credential revocation procedures, replacement or reissuance workflows, and records showing exposed credentials were invalidated within required response windows.
- Owner Primary: Security Engineering
- Owner Supporting: Platform Engineering; Application Development
