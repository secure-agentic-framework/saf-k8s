# SAF-K8S-0404-010 - Post-compromise certificate recovery validation

- Domain: D04 - Identity, Access, and Secrets Management
- Knowledge Area: 4.4 - Certificate Management
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: baseline

## Statement

Organizations must validate service recovery after certificate compromise by confirming affected services are operating correctly on replacement certificates and key pairs.

## Objective

Ensure certificate compromise response restores secure service operation after replacement credentials are deployed.

## Rationale

Recovery validation is independently auditable from the revocation and re-issuance action itself. This control isolates post-replacement validation so teams can verify that services, trust chains, and dependent workloads are actually healthy on the new certificate material.

## Scope

Applies to compromise scenarios affecting API server, etcd, kubelet, webhook, service mesh, and application certificates. Covers service redeployment validation, trust-chain verification, and exercise-based confirmation that services recovered correctly after certificate replacement. Revocation and re-issuance remain governed by SAF-K8S-0404-009.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Service recovery validation after certificate replacement.
- Implementation Surface: Service redeployment validation, trust-chain checks, smoke tests, and exercise records demonstrating successful recovery after certificate compromise response.
- Owner Primary: Platform Engineering
- Owner Supporting: Security Engineering; Security Operations
