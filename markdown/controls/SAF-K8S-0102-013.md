# SAF-K8S-0102-013 - etcd compaction and periodic defragmentation

- Domain: D01 - Control Plane and Cluster Hardening
- Knowledge Area: 1.2 - etcd and Cluster State Protection
- Maturity: Foundational
- Classification: baseline

## Statement

Production Kubernetes clusters must configure automated etcd compaction and run periodic member-safe defragmentation to prevent revision-history growth and storage fragmentation from exhausting quota or degrading control-plane performance.

## Objective

Prevent storage-related denial of service in etcd by controlling revision-history growth and reclaiming storage headroom safely.

## Rationale

Uncompacted revision history and fragmented storage can cause etcd to exceed quota or operate with degraded performance, pushing the cluster toward read-only behavior or instability. Compaction and defragmentation are maintenance controls that preserve safe storage headroom.

## Scope

Applies to all production clusters running self-managed etcd and to managed services where provider compaction and defragmentation settings or SLAs are exposed. Covers auto-compaction retention configuration and sequential defragmentation procedures. Excludes health monitoring and alert generation.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Audit / Telemetry
- Implementation Surface: etcd auto-compaction configuration, scheduled defragmentation automation, and documented operational procedures ensuring members are defragmented sequentially without quorum loss.
- Owner Primary: Platform Engineering
- Owner Supporting: Site Reliability Engineering; Security Operations
