# SAF-K8S-0703-016 - AI workload PriorityClass hierarchy and preemption protection

- Domain: D07 - Storage, Multi-tenancy, and Resource Governance
- Knowledge Area: 7.3 - Resource Governance and Priority
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Organizations must define PriorityClass objects for system-critical, production inference, training, and development AI workload tiers, and must configure preemption policies that preserve inference availability by preventing lower-value or lower-priority workloads from displacing protected serving capacity.

## Objective

Ensure that production inference workloads maintain availability during resource contention by establishing explicit AI workload priority tiers with preemption protection.

## Rationale

Without defined priority tiers, Kubernetes may treat latency-sensitive inference, batch training, and development notebooks as equivalent competitors for scarce GPU capacity. Without explicit preemption behavior, lower-value workloads can crowd out or displace protected inference services during saturation, degrading production availability and creating avoidable service interruptions.

## Scope

Applies to all Kubernetes clusters with GPU resources or dedicated AI workloads. PriorityClass definitions must cover at minimum system-critical, inference-production, training-default, and development-low tiers. Single-tenant dedicated clusters may use simplified hierarchies with documented justification.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: PriorityClass objects and preemptionPolicy settings applied to AI workload tiers.
- Implementation Surface: PriorityClass objects (scheduling.k8s.io/v1) with tiered value hierarchy and preemptionPolicy configuration (PreemptLowerPriority, Never) protecting inference and cluster-critical workloads from lower-priority displacement.
- Owner Primary: Platform Engineering
- Owner Supporting: ML Engineering; FinOps
