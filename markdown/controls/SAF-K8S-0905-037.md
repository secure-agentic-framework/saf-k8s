# SAF-K8S-0905-037 - Manual emergency halt governance and forensic evidence preservation

- Domain: D09 - AI Workload Security: Training, Serving, and Pipelines
- Knowledge Area: 9.5 - AI Supply Chain and Model Lifecycle
- Maturity: Advanced
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Organizations must maintain governed manual emergency halt procedures that authorized operators can invoke within a defined maximum response time for AI workloads exhibiting unsafe, compromised, or non-compliant behavior. Emergency halt procedures must preserve forensic evidence needed for investigation and recovery.

## Objective

Enable human-directed containment of AI workloads through governed emergency halt procedures that preserve evidence for investigation and post-incident analysis.

## Rationale

Not all unsafe AI behavior is captured by automated thresholds. Authorized operators need a reliable manual emergency halt path for ambiguous, emerging, or policy-driven situations, and that halt process must preserve evidence so the event can be investigated and recovered cleanly.

## Scope

Applies to AI training jobs, inference services, and data pipelines where authorized responders may need to halt workloads manually under emergency conditions.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Operator-run emergency halt procedures and supporting evidence-preservation steps.
- Implementation Surface: Emergency halt runbooks, authorized operator procedures, incident-response coordination, evidence preservation workflows, and post-halt forensic collection steps.
- Owner Primary: AI/ML Engineering
- Owner Supporting: Security Operations; SRE
