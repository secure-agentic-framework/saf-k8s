# SAF-K8S-0304-011 - Namespace LimitRange and ResourceQuota enforcement

- Domain: D03 - Workload and Pod Security
- Knowledge Area: 3.4 - Secure Defaults and Resource Constraints
- Maturity: Foundational
- Classification: baseline

## Statement

Every production namespace must enforce LimitRange defaults and maximums together with ResourceQuota aggregate caps for CPU, memory, ephemeral storage, and applicable accelerator resources so that teams cannot bypass resource governance through omitted or excessive specifications.

## Objective

Prevent tenant and namespace level resource exhaustion by enforcing namespace-scoped defaults, ceilings, and aggregate consumption caps.

## Rationale

Pod-level limits alone do not prevent a team from deploying many bounded pods until a namespace or cluster is exhausted. LimitRange establishes default and maximum values, while ResourceQuota constrains aggregate namespace consumption and prevents one tenant from monopolizing shared capacity.

## Scope

Applies to all production namespaces. Accelerator-specific quotas remain applicable where GPU or AI resources are allocated to teams or workloads.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: LimitRange and ResourceQuota admission controllers plus namespace governance reviews and quota utilization monitoring.
- Implementation Surface: LimitRange objects, ResourceQuota objects, and namespace resource consumption dashboards including accelerator quota usage where applicable.
- Owner Primary: Platform Engineering
- Owner Supporting: AI/ML Platform team; Application Development teams

### EU-AI-ACT Article-15.4

- Framework: EU AI Act 2024/1689
- Requirement Title: Accuracy, robustness and cybersecurity
- Relation Type: direct
- Strength: strong
- Applicability: required
- Strength Reason Code: n/a

#### Framework Mapping Notes

High-risk AI systems shall be as resilient as possible regarding errors, faults or inconsistencies that may occur within the system or the environment in which the system operates, in particular due to their interaction with natural persons or other systems. Technical and organisational measures shall be taken in this regard. The robustness of high-risk AI systems may be achieved through technical redundancy solutions, which may include backup or fail-safe plans. High-risk AI systems that continue to learn after being placed on the market or put into service shall be developed in such a way as to eliminate or reduce as far as possible the risk of possibly biased outputs influencing input for future operations (feedback loops), and as to ensure that any such feedback loops are duly addressed with appropriate mitigation measures.

Platform resilience measures include pod disruption budgets, health checks, resource limits, fault-tolerant scheduling, checkpoint security, and infrastructure redundancy -- all governed by SAF-K8S controls.
