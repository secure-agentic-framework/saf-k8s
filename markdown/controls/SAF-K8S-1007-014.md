# SAF-K8S-1007-014 - AI asset classification and criticality governance for Kubernetes environments

- Domain: D10 - Observability, Incident Response, and Governance
- Knowledge Area: 10.7 - Cluster Lifecycle and Asset Inventory
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Organizations must classify AI infrastructure assets on Kubernetes by sensitivity, business criticality, and regulatory exposure, and must maintain those classifications so they can drive risk analysis, response prioritization, and governance decisions.

## Objective

Ensure AI assets carry consistent risk-relevant classification metadata so monitoring, incident response, and governance decisions can be prioritized correctly.

## Rationale

Inventory without classification does not tell defenders which AI assets are most sensitive, business critical, or regulatorily significant. Classification governance enables scoped incident response, criticality-aware vulnerability prioritization, and consistent compliance treatment.

## Scope

Applies to AI infrastructure assets discovered in Kubernetes environments, including models, datasets, pipelines, endpoints, and GPU-backed services.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Classification policy enforcement, metadata governance, and asset review workflows.
- Implementation Surface: Classification label standards, policy-enforced metadata fields, criticality and regulatory-exposure tagging, and asset registry classification records.
- Owner Primary: Platform Engineering
- Owner Supporting: AI/ML Engineering; Security Operations; GRC/Compliance
