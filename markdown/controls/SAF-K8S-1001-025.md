# SAF-K8S-1001-025 - Dual authorization for audit log deletion or modification

- Domain: D10 - Observability, Incident Response, and Governance
- Knowledge Area: 10.1 - Logging and Audit
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: baseline

## Statement

Any approved deletion or modification of audit logs must require dual authorization from at least two authorized individuals, and unilateral audit log purging must be prohibited.

## Objective

Prevent any single administrator or automated process from unilaterally removing or altering audit evidence.

## Rationale

Even when storage protections exist, administrative workflows for deletion, archival changes, or exceptional modification can undermine log integrity if a single actor can approve them. Dual authorization reduces insider risk and makes log tampering materially harder.

## Scope

Applies to administrative and automated workflows that can delete, purge, modify, compact, or otherwise materially affect retained audit logs.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Administrative workflow controls and privileged access governance for audit log lifecycle operations.
- Implementation Surface: Ticketing workflows, privileged access controls, SIEM administration processes, retention exception workflows, and RBAC preventing unilateral log deletion or modification.
- Owner Primary: Security Operations
- Owner Supporting: GRC/Compliance; Identity and Access Management
