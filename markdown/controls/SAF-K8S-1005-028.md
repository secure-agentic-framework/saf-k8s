# SAF-K8S-1005-028 - Model access and training provenance forensic preservation for Kubernetes AI incidents

- Domain: D10 - Observability, Incident Response, and Governance
- Knowledge Area: 10.5 - Incident Response for Kubernetes
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Organizations must extend forensic evidence collection for Kubernetes incidents to preserve model access audit trails and training data, pipeline, or provenance records needed to reconstruct AI workload compromise or misuse.

## Objective

Preserve AI model-access and training-provenance evidence that would otherwise be lost during Kubernetes incident response so investigators can determine whether models, training runs, or data lineage were impacted.

## Rationale

Model access history and training provenance can become inconsistent or disappear quickly during rollback, reruns, or artifact cleanup. Dedicated preservation procedures are required to reconstruct whether a model was tampered with, who accessed it, and whether training data or pipeline lineage was affected.

## Scope

Applies to Kubernetes clusters running model registries, experiment tracking systems, and training or inference pipelines where model access evidence and training provenance are needed for incident reconstruction and post-incident decisions.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: AI-specific forensic playbooks and evidence capture procedures for model access and training provenance artifacts.
- Implementation Surface: Model registry or model CRD access trail preservation, training pipeline execution and data lineage evidence collection, provenance artifact hashing, and storage of model-access and training-provenance forensic outputs in the evidence repository.
- Owner Primary: Security Operations
- Owner Supporting: Digital Forensics; AI/ML Engineering; Legal
