# SAF-K8S-1001-027 - Tamper-resistant retention for Kubernetes AI audit backends

- Domain: D10 - Observability, Incident Response, and Governance
- Knowledge Area: 10.1 - Logging and Audit
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Production Kubernetes clusters running AI workloads must use tamper-resistant retention settings on audit backends so retained audit records cannot be silently altered, prematurely deleted, or lost before the required evidence window expires.

## Objective

Ensure that delivered audit records remain retained and tamper-resistant for the required evidence window after reaching the backend.

## Rationale

Durable delivery is insufficient if attackers or misconfiguration can alter or prematurely purge backend audit records after arrival. Tamper-resistant retention preserves the evidentiary value of audit data by preventing silent overwrites or early deletion within the required retention window.

## Scope

Applies to storage backends receiving Kubernetes and AI workload audit data, including SIEM repositories, logging platforms, object storage, and archival systems. Covers backend-side retention immutability, deletion resistance, and evidence-window preservation. Excludes the transport path that delivers records to the backend.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Audit backend retention and immutability configuration.
- Implementation Surface: Tamper-resistant retention settings for audit backends, immutable or WORM-like storage controls, backend deletion restrictions, and configuration preventing early purge of retained audit data within the required evidence window.
- Owner Primary: Security Operations
- Owner Supporting: Platform Engineering; GRC/Compliance
