# SAF-K8S-0703-017 - PriorityClass assignment restriction and admission enforcement

- Domain: D07 - Storage, Multi-tenancy, and Resource Governance
- Knowledge Area: 7.3 - Resource Governance and Priority
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Organizations must restrict which namespaces, service accounts, and workload types may claim elevated AI workload PriorityClasses, and must enforce admission controls that require approved PriorityClass assignment on pods in AI namespaces.

## Objective

Prevent priority abuse and unauthorized workload escalation by governing who may claim elevated AI workload PriorityClasses and by enforcing approved PriorityClass assignment at admission time.

## Rationale

If workloads can self-assign elevated PriorityClasses without restriction, tenants or users can preempt legitimate inference services, bypass intended scheduling policy, and create cross-tenant denial-of-service conditions. Admission enforcement and assignment restrictions are necessary to ensure the declared priority hierarchy cannot be abused by unauthorized workloads.

## Scope

Applies to all Kubernetes clusters with GPU resources or dedicated AI workloads. Restrictions must cover elevated or protected PriorityClasses used for inference, system-critical services, or other reserved AI workload tiers.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Admission policies and authorization checks governing PriorityClassName assignment on AI workload pods.
- Implementation Surface: Admission control policies requiring approved PriorityClassName values on pods in AI namespaces, plus namespace and service-account restrictions that limit use of protected PriorityClasses to authorized workloads.
- Owner Primary: Platform Engineering
- Owner Supporting: ML Engineering; Security Engineering
