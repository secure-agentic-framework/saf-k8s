# SAF-K8S-0908-009 - Differential privacy parameter governance for inference endpoints

- Domain: D09 - AI Workload Security: Training, Serving, and Pipelines
- Knowledge Area: 9.8 - Model Abuse and Extraction Prevention
- Maturity: Advanced
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Inference endpoints serving models trained on sensitive data must configure differential privacy parameters with documented privacy guarantees so that epsilon, delta, and related calibration choices are approved, reviewable, and aligned to the intended privacy and utility bounds before responses are served.

## Objective

Govern differential privacy parameter selection and formal privacy guarantees for sensitive inference endpoints.

## Rationale

Differential privacy is only defensible when privacy parameters are explicitly selected, documented, and reviewed against the intended guarantees. Without parameter governance, teams can deploy arbitrary epsilon and delta values that overstate privacy claims or silently erode utility and protection.

## Scope

Applies to Kubernetes-hosted inference endpoints serving models trained on sensitive data where formal differential privacy guarantees are required. Covers parameter configuration, guarantee documentation, and validation of the claimed privacy posture. Excludes cumulative privacy budget accounting and threshold enforcement over repeated access.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Differential privacy parameter configuration and guarantee documentation for inference endpoints.
- Implementation Surface: Differential privacy parameter settings per model or endpoint, documented epsilon and delta selections, guarantee review records, and privacy audit validation of the formal privacy claims attached to deployed endpoints.
- Owner Primary: Security Engineering
- Owner Supporting: ML Platform Engineering; Privacy Engineering
