# SAF-K8S-1006-005 - Policy-as-code enforcement for AI workload compliance

- Domain: D10 - Observability, Incident Response, and Governance
- Knowledge Area: 10.6 - Compliance and Governance
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Organizations must implement policy-as-code engines (OPA/Gatekeeper, Kyverno, or Kubewarden) to enforce compliance policies on Kubernetes AI workloads, including image provenance verification, GPU resource limit enforcement, security context constraints, and required classification labels for AI-specific resources.

## Objective

Enforce AI workload compliance requirements at admission and policy evaluation time so non-compliant workloads are prevented from running.

## Rationale

Manual pre-deployment checks do not scale with AI workload velocity. Deterministic admission and policy enforcement reduces security exposure from unsafe images, excessive GPU usage, and insecure runtime settings.

## Scope

Applies to Kubernetes clusters hosting AI workloads. Covers admission and policy enforcement for workload security and governance constraints.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Kubernetes admission control and policy engine evaluation path.
- Implementation Surface: Policy engines, admission policies, workload labels, and namespace policy scopes for AI workloads.
- Owner Primary: GRC/Compliance
- Owner Supporting: Platform Engineering; AI/ML Engineering; Security Engineering
