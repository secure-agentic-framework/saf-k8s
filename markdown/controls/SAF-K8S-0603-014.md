# SAF-K8S-0603-014 - Fail-closed admission enforcement for attestation requirements

- Domain: D06 - Supply Chain, Images, and Admission Control
- Knowledge Area: 6.3 - Attestation, Provenance, and Cryptographic Assurance
- Maturity: Advanced
- Classification: baseline

## Statement

Organizations must enforce policy-based admission decisions that reject artifacts lacking complete verified attestation chains before those artifacts are admitted to deployment environments.

## Objective

Ensure deployment admission fails closed when required attestation evidence is absent, invalid, or incomplete.

## Rationale

Lifecycle verification must culminate in an enforcement point that blocks non-compliant artifacts from entering deployment environments. Admission enforcement provides that fail-closed control and generates auditable rejection evidence when attestation requirements are not met.

## Scope

Applies to Kubernetes admission controllers, deployment gates, and related enforcement points that decide whether artifacts may be admitted to deployment environments based on attestation status.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Kubernetes admission controllers and deployment-time gate enforcement points.
- Implementation Surface: Kyverno or Sigstore policy-controller admission checks, deployment gate decision logic, and rejection logs showing artifacts without complete verified attestation chains are denied before admission.
- Owner Primary: Security Engineering
- Owner Supporting: DevSecOps / Release Engineering; Platform Engineering
