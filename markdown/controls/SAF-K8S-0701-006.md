# SAF-K8S-0701-006 - Dual authorization for retained AI data volume destruction

- Domain: D07 - Storage, Multi-tenancy, and Resource Governance
- Knowledge Area: 7.1 - Persistent Storage Security
- Maturity: Advanced
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Organizations must require dual authorization before destroying retained training data volumes or model checkpoint archives, ensuring that no single account or role can unilaterally delete persistent volumes containing valuable AI artifacts.

## Objective

Prevent unauthorized or accidental destruction of retained AI data volumes by requiring independent approval from two authorized parties before deletion.

## Rationale

Retained volumes containing training data and model checkpoints represent significant compute investment and irreplaceable artifacts. A single compromised or mistaken account that can unilaterally delete these volumes creates an unacceptable risk of permanent data loss. Dual authorization ensures that volume destruction requires deliberate, independently verified intent from two parties.

## Scope

Applies to all retained PersistentVolumes storing training data backups and model checkpoint archives. The dual authorization mechanism must be enforced at the storage backend or through an admission control webhook that intercepts PV deletion requests. Scratch volumes and ephemeral training caches are excluded.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Admission control webhooks or storage backend policies requiring dual authorization for PV deletion requests on retained AI data volumes.
- Implementation Surface: Custom admission webhooks intercepting PV deletion requests and requiring multi-person approval, cloud provider deletion protection settings, audit logging for volume lifecycle events, and operational procedures for retained volume destruction with multi-person approval.
- Owner Primary: Security Engineering
- Owner Supporting: Platform Engineering; Data Governance
