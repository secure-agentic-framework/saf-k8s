# SAF-K8S-1005-026 - Forensic chain-of-custody and evidence handling for Kubernetes AI incidents

- Domain: D10 - Observability, Incident Response, and Governance
- Knowledge Area: 10.5 - Incident Response for Kubernetes
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Organizations must establish documented chain-of-custody procedures and evidence handling controls for forensic artifacts collected during Kubernetes AI incidents.

## Objective

Preserve the integrity, traceability, and admissibility of forensic artifacts collected during Kubernetes AI incidents through defensible custody and evidence handling procedures.

## Rationale

Collected evidence loses value if responders cannot show how it was handled, transferred, stored, and protected from tampering. Kubernetes AI investigations require explicit custody records and evidence handling workflows to support investigation, legal review, and regulator scrutiny.

## Scope

Applies to Kubernetes clusters hosting AI workloads where forensic readiness is required. Covers custody documentation, evidence transfer records, storage controls, and chain-of-custody handling for forensic artifacts collected during incident response.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Chain-of-custody workflow and evidence handling procedures for forensic artifacts.
- Implementation Surface: Custody documentation templates, evidence handling logs, transfer records, evidence repository controls, and procedures for preserving the integrity of collected forensic artifacts.
- Owner Primary: Security Operations
- Owner Supporting: Digital Forensics; AI/ML Engineering; Legal
