# SAF-K8S-1001-019 - Regulatory AI artifact and provenance record retention enforcement

- Domain: D10 - Observability, Incident Response, and Governance
- Knowledge Area: 10.1 - Logging and Audit
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Organizations subject to regulatory requirements must enforce retention policies for AI model artifacts, provenance records, human oversight decision records, and related compliance evidence so those artifacts are preserved for the required period and protected from premature lifecycle deletion.

## Objective

Ensure regulatory-required AI artifacts and provenance evidence remain preserved for audit, monitoring, and post-market obligations.

## Rationale

Regulatory retention duties extend beyond logs to model artifacts, provenance records, and oversight evidence. If storage lifecycle rules delete those artifacts too early, the organization cannot reconstruct model lineage, oversight decisions, or post-market evidence during audits or incident response.

## Scope

Applies to retained AI model versions, provenance records, training or deployment lineage artifacts, and human oversight decision evidence stored in registries, object stores, or compliance repositories. Excludes general audit-log retention.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Artifact storage lifecycle policy and compliance retention scanning.
- Implementation Surface: Object storage lifecycle policies, registry retention protection for referenced artifacts, provenance-record retention configuration, human-oversight evidence retention rules, and automated compliance checks preventing premature deletion of retained AI artifacts.
- Owner Primary: GRC/Compliance
- Owner Supporting: Platform Engineering; Security Engineering
