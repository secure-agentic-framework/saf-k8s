# SAF-K8S-1004-006 - Cross-source threat correlation with business context for AI incidents

- Domain: D10 - Observability, Incident Response, and Governance
- Knowledge Area: 10.4 - AI and Supply Chain Threat Taxonomy
- Maturity: Advanced
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Organizations must correlate technical audit data with business context and non-technical sources, including organizational risk indicators, physical security events, and HR actions, to support comprehensive threat assessment and incident prioritization for Kubernetes AI environments.

## Objective

Improve threat assessment and incident prioritization by correlating technical Kubernetes AI telemetry with business-context and non-technical risk signals.

## Rationale

Technical telemetry alone often fails to distinguish routine noise from targeted insider activity, coordinated abuse, or incidents with elevated business impact. Correlating audit data with organizational and non-technical context helps analysts prioritize the right incidents and assess whether suspicious activity is isolated, systemic, or business-critical.

## Scope

Applies to security operations teams responsible for Kubernetes AI infrastructure and incident triage. Covers cross-source correlation workflows that join technical telemetry with business context and non-technical signals during threat assessment and investigation.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Cross-source correlation workflow in SIEM or SOAR and incident triage process.
- Implementation Surface: Correlation of technical audit data with organizational risk indicators, physical security events, HR actions, and other non-technical context; SIEM or SOAR enrichment workflows; analyst triage playbooks; and incident assessment procedures that incorporate contextual signals into prioritization and escalation.
- Owner Primary: Security Operations
- Owner Supporting: Threat Intelligence; Detection Engineering; Security Architecture
