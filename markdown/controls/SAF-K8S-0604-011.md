# SAF-K8S-0604-011 - SLSA provenance generation and target-level governance

- Domain: D06 - Supply Chain, Images, and Admission Control
- Knowledge Area: 6.4 - SBOMs and Vulnerability Intelligence
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: baseline

## Statement

Build pipelines must generate SLSA provenance for deployable artifacts at the target SLSA level defined by organizational policy, and the required target level and improvement roadmap must be documented, reviewed, and verifiable through policy or admission checks before artifacts are trusted downstream.

## Objective

Establish auditable provenance records and an explicit SLSA assurance target so the organization can prove how each artifact was built and what provenance threshold governed its acceptance.

## Rationale

Without standardized SLSA provenance and a documented target assurance level, organizations cannot reliably distinguish approved builds from artifacts produced by tampered or weakly controlled pipelines. Provenance generation plus target-level governance makes build trust decisions explicit, reviewable, and enforceable.

## Scope

Applies to all CI/CD pipelines producing deployable Kubernetes artifacts, including application images, AI workload images, and infrastructure operator images. The target SLSA level and roadmap must be reviewed at least annually or when pipeline risk materially changes.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: CI/CD provenance generation step and provenance policy verification gate.
- Implementation Surface: SLSA provenance generation tooling, provenance attachment to image digests, documented target SLSA level policy, roadmap records, and admission or policy-engine verification of required provenance level.
- Owner Primary: DevSecOps / Release Engineering
- Owner Supporting: Security Engineering; Platform Engineering
