# SAF-K8S-0405-024 - Break-glass credential automatic expiration and revocation enforcement

- Domain: D04 - Identity, Access, and Secrets Management
- Knowledge Area: 4.5 - Identity Abuse Detection and Mitigation
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: baseline

## Statement

Organizations must ensure break-glass credentials, kubeconfigs, or certificates are automatically time-limited, revoked, or expired after the emergency window so emergency access cannot persist as standing privileged access.

## Objective

Ensure emergency credentials are automatically retired after use so break-glass access does not persist beyond the approved recovery window.

## Rationale

Temporary emergency access becomes dangerous if it remains valid after the incident. Automatic expiration and revocation reduce the risk that exceptional privileges silently become durable administrative backdoors.

## Scope

Applies to all break-glass credentials, kubeconfigs, or certificates issued for production Kubernetes clusters, including managed and multi-tenant environments. Covers audit trails, post-use review evidence, automatic revocation, and expiration after emergency access use.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Emergency credential expiry and revocation controls.
- Implementation Surface: Short TTLs for emergency credentials, automatic revocation or expiration policies, and validation that break-glass access cannot persist after the approved emergency window.
- Owner Primary: Security Engineering
- Owner Supporting: Platform Engineering; Identity and Access Management
