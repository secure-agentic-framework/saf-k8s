# SAF-K8S-0603-011 - Attestation policy definition, signing, and change governance

- Domain: D06 - Supply Chain, Images, and Admission Control
- Knowledge Area: 6.3 - Attestation, Provenance, and Cryptographic Assurance
- Maturity: Advanced
- Classification: baseline

## Statement

Organizations must define signed attestation policies that encode artifact requirements for each pipeline stage, and those policies must be versioned, signed, and subject to documented change control before they are used to govern artifact promotion or deployment.

## Objective

Ensure attestation requirements are defined in a controlled, reviewable policy artifact so the organization can prove what rules governed artifact trust decisions and who approved policy changes.

## Rationale

If attestation requirements are undocumented, unsigned, or weakly governed, operators cannot prove which controls should have been enforced at a given pipeline stage and attackers can weaken policy content without reliable detection. Signed, versioned policy governance makes downstream trust decisions auditable and resistant to tampering.

## Scope

Applies to all CI/CD pipelines and admission controllers that rely on attestation requirements. Policy definitions must be stored in version-controlled repositories or equivalent controlled stores with branch protection and change approval.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Policy authoring and approval workflow for attestation requirements.
- Implementation Surface: Signed attestation policy documents, OCI-stored or repository-stored policy artifacts, version control protections, and change-management workflows for attestation policy updates.
- Owner Primary: Security Engineering
- Owner Supporting: DevSecOps / Release Engineering; Platform Engineering
