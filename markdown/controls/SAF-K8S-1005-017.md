# SAF-K8S-1005-017 - Recovery-time lateral movement containment for compromised AI workloads

- Domain: D10 - Observability, Incident Response, and Governance
- Knowledge Area: 10.5 - Incident Response for Kubernetes
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Organizations must enforce recovery-time controls that prevent compromised AI workloads from moving laterally to the cluster control plane or other tenants during and after incident recovery.

## Objective

Ensure compromised AI workloads cannot pivot into cluster control plane components or co-tenant environments while recovery operations are in progress.

## Rationale

Compromised AI workloads with elevated privileges, GPU access, or mounted storage can pivot to control plane components or other tenants if recovery does not preserve strict isolation. Recovery procedures must contain the blast radius until affected workloads are verified and stabilized.

## Scope

Applies to all Kubernetes clusters recovering from security incidents affecting AI workloads. Covers RBAC, network segmentation, namespace isolation, and credential controls used to contain compromised AI workloads during recovery.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Recovery runbooks, RBAC policy review, NetworkPolicy enforcement, namespace isolation, and credential rotation controls for compromised AI workloads.
- Implementation Surface: RBAC least-privilege verification for AI workload ServiceAccounts, NetworkPolicy segmentation between AI workloads and the control plane, namespace isolation for compromised workloads, and credential rotation for secrets accessible from compromised components.
- Owner Primary: Security Operations
- Owner Supporting: Platform Engineering; AI/ML Engineering
