# SAF-K8S-0401-002 - RBAC permission audit and analysis

- Domain: D04 - Identity, Access, and Secrets Management
- Knowledge Area: 4.1 - Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)
- Maturity: Foundational
- Classification: baseline

## Statement

Organizations must perform periodic RBAC permission audits to identify overly permissive roles, detect privilege escalation paths, and remediate findings using RBAC analysis tooling such as rakkess, kubectl-who-can, or equivalent.

## Objective

Continuously verify that RBAC configurations remain aligned with least-privilege principles and that no unintended privilege escalation paths have been introduced through role changes or new bindings.

## Rationale

RBAC configurations drift over time as teams add roles, bindings, and exceptions to meet operational needs. Without regular audits, permissions accumulate beyond what is required, creating hidden privilege escalation paths that attackers can exploit. Automated tooling detects issues such as subjects who can create RoleBindings referencing cluster-admin or who hold escalate, bind, or impersonate verbs without justification.

## Scope

Applies to all Kubernetes clusters hosting production or sensitive workloads. Audits must cover all namespaces including kube-system and operator-managed namespaces. Managed service environments must audit all customer-configurable RBAC objects.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: RBAC audit tooling integrated into CI/CD pipelines or scheduled cluster scans, with findings tracked in the organization's vulnerability management system.
- Implementation Surface: RBAC analysis tools (rakkess, kubectl-who-can, rbac-tool), policy engine audit modes (Kyverno audit, Gatekeeper dry-run), CI/CD pipeline RBAC manifest analysis, privilege escalation path detection for escalate/bind/impersonate verbs, SIEM integration for RBAC audit findings, and vulnerability management system tracking.
- Owner Primary: Security Engineering
- Owner Supporting: Platform Engineering; Identity and Access Management
