# SAF-K8S-1005-021 - Kubernetes containment runbooks for node draining, namespace isolation, and workload suspension

- Domain: D10 - Observability, Incident Response, and Governance
- Knowledge Area: 10.5 - Incident Response for Kubernetes
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Organizations must define and document Kubernetes incident containment procedures for node draining, namespace isolation, and workload suspension, with pre-built runbooks and isolation templates that enable targeted containment while minimizing impact to unaffected workloads.

## Objective

Enable rapid, targeted platform-level containment of Kubernetes incidents through documented and tested node, namespace, and workload isolation procedures that reduce blast radius without causing unnecessary disruption.

## Rationale

Kubernetes incidents require targeted containment actions such as node draining, namespace isolation, and workload suspension that must be executed quickly and consistently. Without documented runbooks and isolation templates, responders may over-isolate and cause avoidable outages or under-isolate and fail to contain the incident.

## Scope

Applies to all Kubernetes clusters hosting AI workloads. Covers node-level, namespace-level, and workload-level containment procedures, including multi-tenant environments where unaffected workloads must remain available wherever possible.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: IR playbook containment procedures, namespace isolation templates, and emergency authorization for Kubernetes containment actions.
- Implementation Surface: kubectl cordon and drain procedures, deny-all NetworkPolicy manifests with documented exceptions, workload suspension or scaling procedures, and tested runbooks for each platform containment layer.
- Owner Primary: Security Operations
- Owner Supporting: Platform Engineering; AI/ML Engineering; SRE
