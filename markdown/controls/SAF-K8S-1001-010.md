# SAF-K8S-1001-010 - Kubernetes audit level and stage filtering for AI workloads

- Domain: D10 - Observability, Incident Response, and Governance
- Knowledge Area: 10.1 - Logging and Audit
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Production Kubernetes clusters running AI workloads must configure appropriate Kubernetes audit levels and stage filtering for AI-relevant resources so that security-critical events are recorded with sufficient detail while low-value noise is reduced.

## Objective

Ensure that audit records for AI-relevant activity contain the level of detail needed for investigation without overwhelming the audit subsystem with unnecessary event volume.

## Rationale

Audit coverage alone is insufficient if the recorded event detail is too shallow for reconstruction or if noisy stages obscure important signals. AI workloads often require different audit levels for inference services, training jobs, secrets, and long-running requests.

## Scope

Applies to Kubernetes audit policy tuning for AI workloads, including inference services, training job controllers, secrets, admission decisions, and long-running API requests where stage visibility matters.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Audit policy level selection and stage filtering in kube-apiserver audit policy configuration.
- Implementation Surface: Audit policy rule ordering, level selection (Metadata, Request, RequestResponse), stage filters such as ResponseComplete and ResponseStarted, and AI-resource-specific tuning for inference, training, and authorization-sensitive resources.
- Owner Primary: Security Operations
- Owner Supporting: Platform Engineering; AI/ML Engineering
