# SAF-K8S-0604-009 - AI workload vulnerability prioritization and remediation SLAs

- Domain: D06 - Supply Chain, Images, and Admission Control
- Knowledge Area: 6.4 - SBOMs and Vulnerability Intelligence
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Vulnerability management processes must prioritize AI workload findings according to workload context, exploitability, and exposure profile, and must enforce differentiated remediation SLAs for each AI workload tier.

## Objective

Ensure AI workload vulnerabilities are remediated in risk-ranked order with workload-tier-specific response expectations.

## Rationale

Even after findings are correctly classified, organizations still need a defensible prioritization model and explicit remediation timelines. Without differentiated SLAs, critical inference-tier vulnerabilities compete with lower-risk training-only findings and patching decisions drift toward generic CVSS sorting instead of workload-specific business impact and exposure.

## Scope

Applies to all AI and ML workload images deployed in Kubernetes clusters, including inference servers, training jobs, data preprocessing pipelines, and model-serving infrastructure. Covers prioritization criteria, workload-tier risk ranking, and remediation SLA assignment. Does not cover exposure classification mechanics or automated rebuild pipeline execution, which are addressed by SAF-K8S-0604-008 and SAF-K8S-0604-010.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Vulnerability management platform prioritization policy configuration and workload-tier SLA enforcement rules.
- Implementation Surface: Prioritization rules that weight exploitability context, blast radius, internet exposure, and workload criticality; differentiated remediation SLA definitions for training, inference, and supporting AI pipeline tiers; exception workflows for deferred remediation; and reporting that measures remediation timelines by workload category against SLA targets.
- Owner Primary: Security Engineering
- Owner Supporting: ML Platform Engineering; DevSecOps / Release Engineering
