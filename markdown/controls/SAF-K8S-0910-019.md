# SAF-K8S-0910-019 - Compromised-cluster federation isolation and re-admission governance

- Domain: D09 - AI Workload Security: Training, Serving, and Pipelines
- Knowledge Area: 9.10 - Multi-Cluster and Federated AI Security
- Maturity: Advanced
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Multi-cluster AI workload orchestration control planes must implement circuit-breaker controls that isolate compromised clusters from the federation, prevent new placement or continued execution where required, and require governed approval before isolated clusters are re-admitted.

## Objective

Contain compromised member clusters before they can continue receiving workloads or influencing the wider federation, and ensure controlled re-entry after remediation.

## Rationale

Even with strong placement gating, a cluster can become unsafe after admission to the federation. Circuit-breaker isolation limits blast radius by stopping further placement and federation participation, while governed re-admission ensures clusters do not silently rejoin before remediation and review are complete.

## Scope

Applies to organizations operating AI workloads across multiple Kubernetes clusters, whether on-premises, multi-cloud, or hybrid. Covers circuit-breaker isolation triggers, federation removal or workload draining actions, and approval-based re-admission of previously isolated clusters. Excludes placement-time posture verification before initial workload routing.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Federation isolation trigger configuration, compromised-cluster isolation actions, and re-admission approval workflow.
- Implementation Surface: Circuit-breaker isolation triggers, federation credential revocation or workload draining workflows, compromised-cluster quarantine controls, manual override handling, and re-admission approval records for isolated clusters.
- Owner Primary: Platform Engineering
- Owner Supporting: ML Platform Engineering; Security Engineering; SRE
