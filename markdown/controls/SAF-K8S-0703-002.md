# SAF-K8S-0703-002 - Pod Disruption Budgets for workload availability

- Domain: D07 - Storage, Multi-tenancy, and Resource Governance
- Knowledge Area: 7.3 - Resource Governance and Priority
- Maturity: Foundational
- Classification: baseline

## Statement

Organizations must configure PodDisruptionBudget objects for all production workloads and stateful AI services to define minimum availability or maximum unavailable pod counts during voluntary disruptions such as node maintenance, cluster upgrades, and node pool scaling operations.

## Objective

Maintain workload availability during planned disruptions by preventing the scheduler from evicting more pods than the service can tolerate at any given time.

## Rationale

Without PodDisruptionBudgets, node drain operations during maintenance or upgrades can simultaneously evict all replicas of a service, causing a complete outage. For AI inference services, this means request failures and SLA violations. For stateful training workloads, disruption can cause checkpoint loss and wasted compute hours. PDBs give the scheduler a contract about minimum availability, ensuring graceful disruption handling.

## Scope

Applies to all production deployments, statefulsets, and long-running AI workloads (inference services, model serving endpoints, distributed training jobs) across all Kubernetes clusters. Batch training jobs with checkpointing may have relaxed PDB settings. Development and ephemeral workloads may be excluded with documented justification.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: PodDisruptionBudget objects associated with deployments and statefulsets, validated by admission control policies that require PDBs for production-labeled workloads.
- Implementation Surface: PodDisruptionBudget (policy/v1) objects with minAvailable and maxUnavailable specifications, label selectors targeting inference services and stateful training workloads, percentage-based minAvailable for autoscaled services, node drain and cluster upgrade coordination, and documentation of operational trade-offs for maxUnavailable: 0 on non-interruptible training jobs.
- Owner Primary: Platform Engineering
- Owner Supporting: ML Engineering; SRE
