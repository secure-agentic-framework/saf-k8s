# SAF-K8S-0104-015 - CIS Benchmark result retention and posture trend reporting

- Domain: D01 - Control Plane and Cluster Hardening
- Knowledge Area: 1.4 - CIS Benchmarks and Patch Management
- Maturity: Foundational
- Classification: baseline

## Statement

Production Kubernetes clusters must retain recurring CIS Kubernetes Benchmark assessment results and produce posture trend reporting over time so that hardening status, drift, and sustained baseline assurance can be demonstrated during operations, audits, and risk reviews.

## Objective

Demonstrate benchmark-derived hardening posture over time by preserving scan history and producing trend evidence that shows configuration drift or sustained compliance.

## Rationale

Running scans without retaining historical results leaves teams unable to prove sustained hardening, identify slow configuration drift, or support audit and risk review. This control isolates the evidence-retention and trend-reporting obligation from scan execution itself.

## Scope

Applies to all production clusters subject to recurring CIS Benchmark assessment. Covers retained scan history, evidence repositories, and reporting that shows benchmark posture over time.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Audit Evidence Retention
- Implementation Surface: Evidence repository for benchmark outputs, reporting pipeline, and posture trend dashboards or reports.
- Owner Primary: Security Engineering
- Owner Supporting: Platform Engineering; Governance Risk and Compliance

#### Framework Mapping Notes

High-risk AI systems shall be resilient against attempts by unauthorised third parties to alter their use, outputs or performance by exploiting system vulnerabilities. The technical solutions aiming to ensure the cybersecurity of high-risk AI systems shall be appropriate to the relevant circumstances and the risks. The technical solutions to address AI specific vulnerabilities shall include, where appropriate, measures to prevent, detect, respond to, resolve and control for attacks trying to manipulate the training data set (data poisoning), or pre-trained components used in training (model poisoning), inputs designed to cause the AI model to make a mistake (adversarial examples or model evasion), confidentiality attacks or model flaws.

Risk-appropriate cybersecurity is the fundamental SAF-K8S design principle. Controls are tiered by maturity level and archetype to match circumstances and risk profiles.
