# SAF-K8S-0903-008 - Budget-aware inference autoscaling suppression and degraded-service fallback

- Domain: D09 - AI Workload Security: Training, Serving, and Pipelines
- Knowledge Area: 9.3 - Inference Resilience, Adversarial Defense, and Resource Controls
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

GPU-aware autoscaling for inference workloads must integrate budget or spend thresholds that suppress further scale-up and invoke bounded degraded-service behavior when projected inference cost exceeds approved limits.

## Objective

Prevent inference denial-of-wallet by linking autoscaling decisions to approved budget thresholds and controlled degraded-service responses.

## Rationale

Replica ceilings alone do not prevent economically abusive scale-out if the allowed ceiling itself is financially unsafe. Budget-aware suppression provides a second safety layer that disables additional scale-up or shifts service to bounded fallback behavior when spending thresholds are crossed, reducing the blast radius of sustained adversarial demand.

## Scope

Applies to autoscaled inference deployments on Kubernetes that use cloud GPU capacity or other billable elastic accelerators. Covers budget-threshold integration, autoscaler suppression logic, and bounded degraded-service responses such as request throttling or queueing. Excludes general team or namespace spend governance, which remains on existing GPU budget controls.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Autoscaler budget integration, spend policy engine, and degraded-service decision logic in the inference path.
- Implementation Surface: Budget-policy outputs linked to autoscaler behavior, cloud spend or projected-cost thresholds that disable further scale-out, and degraded-service automation such as bounded queueing, throttling, or HTTP 429 responses when cost limits are reached.
- Owner Primary: ML Platform Engineering
- Owner Supporting: Platform Engineering; FinOps
