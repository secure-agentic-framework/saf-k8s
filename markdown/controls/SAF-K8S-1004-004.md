# SAF-K8S-1004-004 - Kubernetes AI threat intelligence feed ingestion and detection enrichment

- Domain: D10 - Observability, Incident Response, and Governance
- Knowledge Area: 10.4 - AI and Supply Chain Threat Taxonomy
- Maturity: Advanced
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Organizations must consume Kubernetes CVE feeds, runtime threat intelligence, and relevant external threat-sharing sources for Kubernetes AI environments, and must use that intelligence to develop, maintain, and prioritize detection rules and exposure assessments.

## Objective

Keep Kubernetes AI detection and exposure analysis current by continuously ingesting actionable external threat intelligence and translating it into operational detections and triage inputs.

## Rationale

Kubernetes AI environments are affected by rapidly changing vulnerability disclosures, runtime threat indicators, and ecosystem-wide attacker tradecraft. Without disciplined feed ingestion and detection enrichment, defenders miss emerging exposure, detection logic becomes stale, and response teams lose time re-deriving threat context during active incidents.

## Scope

Applies to security operations teams responsible for Kubernetes AI infrastructure. Covers external feed ingestion, threat-sharing participation, rule enrichment, and vulnerability exposure assessment for AI platforms, clusters, runtimes, and supporting components.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Threat intelligence platform configuration, detection rule management system, and feed-driven exposure assessment workflow in SIEM or SOAR.
- Implementation Surface: Kubernetes CVE feed consumption, NVIDIA and platform security bulletin monitoring, runtime threat intelligence feeds, automated or analyst-driven threat-sharing intake, SIEM detection rule development from threat intelligence, indicator ingestion, and enrichment workflows for vulnerability and attack exposure assessment.
- Owner Primary: Security Operations
- Owner Supporting: Threat Intelligence; Detection Engineering; Security Architecture
