# SAF-K8S-1001-034 - Centralized AI log backend immutability and integrity verification

- Domain: D10 - Observability, Incident Response, and Governance
- Knowledge Area: 10.1 - Logging and Audit
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: baseline

## Statement

Organizations must protect centralized AI workload log backends against tampering through cryptographic verification, append-only immutability, or equivalent integrity controls so retained log evidence remains trustworthy for audit and investigation.

## Objective

Preserve the integrity and evidentiary trustworthiness of centralized AI workload logs through backend immutability or cryptographic verification controls.

## Rationale

Centralized collection does not preserve evidentiary value if logs can be altered or truncated after ingestion. Backend immutability and integrity verification ensure centralized AI log evidence remains trustworthy for incident response, audit, and regulatory review.

## Scope

Applies to centralized logging backends for AI workloads, including audit logs, training and inference logs, GPU hardware logs, and platform security telemetry retained for operational or regulatory purposes. Covers append-only storage, immutable backend settings, signed log batches, and integrity verification mechanisms. Excludes retention period assignment and lifecycle enforcement.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Centralized log backend immutability and integrity verification controls.
- Implementation Surface: Append-only or WORM-compliant storage, signed log batches or hash-chain integrity verification, immutable object storage controls, and periodic validation that centralized logs remain unmodified and trustworthy.
- Owner Primary: Security Operations
- Owner Supporting: Platform Engineering; Infrastructure Engineering
