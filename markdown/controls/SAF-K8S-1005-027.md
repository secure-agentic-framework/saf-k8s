# SAF-K8S-1005-027 - GPU and accelerator forensic evidence preservation for Kubernetes AI incidents

- Domain: D10 - Observability, Incident Response, and Governance
- Knowledge Area: 10.5 - Incident Response for Kubernetes
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Organizations must extend forensic evidence collection for Kubernetes incidents to preserve AI-specific GPU allocation, accelerator state, and related hardware telemetry evidence needed to reconstruct compromise or misuse of accelerator-backed AI workloads.

## Objective

Preserve volatile GPU and accelerator evidence that would otherwise be lost during Kubernetes incident response so investigators can determine whether accelerator-backed AI workloads or hardware state were impacted.

## Rationale

GPU and accelerator evidence is highly volatile and often disappears quickly during node recycling, workload restart, or device plugin state changes. Dedicated accelerator-focused preservation procedures are required to reconstruct misuse, persistence, or tampering involving specialized hardware resources.

## Scope

Applies to Kubernetes clusters running GPU or accelerator-backed AI workloads where accelerator telemetry, allocation state, or device-specific evidence is needed for incident reconstruction and post-incident decisions.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: AI-specific forensic playbooks and evidence capture procedures for accelerator and GPU artifacts.
- Implementation Surface: GPU telemetry and allocation evidence capture, accelerator state preservation, device-plugin and node-level accelerator evidence collection, artifact hashing, and storage of accelerator forensic outputs in the evidence repository.
- Owner Primary: Security Operations
- Owner Supporting: Digital Forensics; AI/ML Engineering; Legal
