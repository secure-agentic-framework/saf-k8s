# SAF-K8S-1005-001 - Kubernetes incident response lifecycle

- Domain: D10 - Observability, Incident Response, and Governance
- Knowledge Area: 10.5 - Incident Response for Kubernetes
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Organizations must implement a complete incident response lifecycle for Kubernetes environments covering preparation, detection and analysis, containment, eradication and recovery, and post-incident activity.

## Objective

Ensure that incident response processes are tailored to Kubernetes operational patterns across all lifecycle phases, from preparation through post-incident activity.

## Rationale

Standard enterprise incident response procedures do not address Kubernetes-specific lifecycle phases such as pod-aware detection, namespace-scoped containment, and workload-specific eradication steps. Without a Kubernetes-tailored IR lifecycle, responders apply generic procedures that increase mean time to containment and risk losing forensic evidence through incorrect pod deletion or node actions during the eradication phase.

## Scope

Applies to all Kubernetes clusters hosting AI workloads in production. Covers the full IR lifecycle with Kubernetes-specific procedures at each phase. Lifecycle procedures must be tested through tabletop or live exercises at least annually.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: IR playbook repository, SOC runbook management system, and exercise/drill scheduling process.
- Implementation Surface: NIST SP 800-61r2 IR lifecycle implementation, Kubernetes-specific forensic tooling (kubectl debug with ephemeral containers, container filesystem snapshot capability, audit log export scripts), pre-staged NetworkPolicy containment templates, out-of-band communication channels, SIEM correlation rule triggers, and severity classification criteria for AI-specific incident types.
- Owner Primary: Security Operations
- Owner Supporting: AI/ML Engineering; Platform Engineering; Legal
