# SAF-K8S-0901-004 - Training fault tolerance and security

- Domain: D09 - AI Workload Security: Training, Serving, and Pipelines
- Knowledge Area: 9.1 - Distributed Training Workload Security
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Training fault tolerance mechanisms must include corrupted data detection at recovery points, hardened retry logic that prevents adversary manipulation, and verified backup and rollback strategies for training state that ensure recovery only from integrity-checked snapshots.

## Objective

Ensure that fault tolerance and recovery mechanisms for training jobs cannot be exploited by adversaries to inject corrupted data or manipulate model state during error recovery.

## Rationale

Distributed training jobs rely on fault tolerance mechanisms such as automatic retries and checkpoint recovery to handle infrastructure failures. An adversary can exploit retry logic by deliberately causing failures and injecting corrupted data during recovery, or by tampering with checkpoints so that recovery loads a poisoned model state. Without integrity verification at recovery points, these attacks are invisible to the training system.

## Scope

Applies to all fault tolerance, retry, and recovery mechanisms in distributed training orchestration on Kubernetes. Covers training operator retry policies, checkpoint-based recovery, and data re-ingestion during fault handling. Excludes general Kubernetes pod restart policies unrelated to training state.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Training operator retry and recovery configuration, checkpoint integrity verification hooks, and training job controller settings.
- Implementation Surface: Training operator retry and recovery configuration (backoffLimit on Kubernetes Jobs), checkpoint integrity verification hooks (SHA-256 hash verification before restore), validation pass scripts for post-recovery model evaluation, backup storage diversification across PersistentVolumes and object storage, and training job controller fault-tolerance settings.
- Owner Primary: ML Platform Engineering
- Owner Supporting: Security Engineering; ML Engineering

- Requirement Title: Accuracy, robustness and cybersecurity
- Relation Type: direct
- Strength: strong
- Applicability: required
- Strength Reason Code: n/a

#### Framework Mapping Notes

High-risk AI systems shall be as resilient as possible regarding errors, faults or inconsistencies that may occur within the system or the environment in which the system operates, in particular due to their interaction with natural persons or other systems. Technical and organisational measures shall be taken in this regard. The robustness of high-risk AI systems may be achieved through technical redundancy solutions, which may include backup or fail-safe plans. High-risk AI systems that continue to learn after being placed on the market or put into service shall be developed in such a way as to eliminate or reduce as far as possible the risk of possibly biased outputs influencing input for future operations (feedback loops), and as to ensure that any such feedback loops are duly addressed with appropriate mitigation measures.

Kubernetes provides infrastructure redundancy through replica sets, multi-zone scheduling, PDBs, etcd backups, and disaster recovery controls (SAF-K8S D1, D7, D10).
