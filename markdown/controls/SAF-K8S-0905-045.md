# SAF-K8S-0905-045 - Sandboxed external model behavioral vetting and disposition review

- Domain: D09 - AI Workload Security: Training, Serving, and Pipelines
- Knowledge Area: 9.5 - AI Supply Chain and Model Lifecycle
- Maturity: Advanced
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Organizations must perform sandboxed execution or behavioral vetting of externally sourced models before internal use and must document pass, fail, reject, or escalated disposition decisions based on that vetting.

## Objective

Detect unsafe or backdoored model behavior that static scanning cannot reveal by requiring sandboxed or behavioral vetting before internal approval.

## Rationale

Static scanning cannot detect every malicious or unsafe model behavior. Sandboxed execution and behavioral vetting are needed to observe suspicious runtime characteristics, degraded model behavior, or policy violations before public models are admitted for fine-tuning or serving.

## Scope

Applies to all models sourced from public hubs and community repositories after initial quarantine intake. Covers sandbox execution, behavioral checks, and documented disposition decisions. Excludes source allowlist governance and internal re-signing after approval.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Sandbox execution and behavioral review workflow for candidate external models.
- Implementation Surface: Sandboxed execution with monitoring, behavioral validation against standard datasets, analyst review, and workflow records showing pass, fail, reject, or escalation decisions.
- Owner Primary: Security Engineering
- Owner Supporting: ML Platform Engineering; ML Engineering
