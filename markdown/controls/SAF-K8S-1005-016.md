# SAF-K8S-1005-016 - Documented post-incident model retraining or rollback decisions

- Domain: D10 - Observability, Incident Response, and Governance
- Knowledge Area: 10.5 - Incident Response for Kubernetes
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Organizations must make and document retraining, rollback, quarantine, or related recovery decisions for AI models based on post-incident integrity verification results so model recovery actions are justified, reviewable, and consistently executed.

## Objective

Ensure AI model recovery after infrastructure compromise is governed by documented retraining or rollback decisions derived from integrity verification results.

## Rationale

Model integrity validation alone is insufficient if recovery teams do not convert validation results into a documented decision about whether to retrain, roll back, quarantine, or reapprove a model. Unstructured recovery decisions can return compromised or untrustworthy models to service.

## Scope

Applies to AI workloads in Kubernetes clusters affected by a security incident. Covers documented retraining or rollback decision frameworks, quarantine or rollback procedures, and justification records tied to model recovery outcomes. Excludes the integrity verification mechanics that produce the underlying validation evidence.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Incident response recovery decision governance and model recovery procedures.
- Implementation Surface: Documented model recovery decision frameworks, quarantine or rollback procedures, retraining decision records, approval criteria for returning a model to service, and justification artifacts tied to post-incident integrity validation outcomes.
- Owner Primary: Security Operations
- Owner Supporting: AI/ML Engineering; Digital Forensics
