# SAF-K8S-1003-002 - OCTAVE risk-based threat assessment for Kubernetes AI environments

- Domain: D10 - Observability, Incident Response, and Governance
- Knowledge Area: 10.3 - Threat Modeling Methodologies
- Maturity: Advanced
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Organizations must perform OCTAVE-based risk assessments for Kubernetes AI environments, identifying critical AI assets (models, training data, GPU infrastructure, inference endpoints), profiling threats specific to those assets, and prioritizing risk treatment based on organizational risk tolerance and business impact.

## Objective

Ensure that threat assessment for Kubernetes AI infrastructure is grounded in organizational risk context, asset criticality, and business impact rather than purely technical vulnerability scanning.

## Rationale

Technical vulnerability assessments alone do not capture the business risk posed by AI asset compromise, such as the impact of a proprietary model being extracted or training data being poisoned. OCTAVE's asset-centric, risk-based approach ensures that security investments are aligned with the actual business value of AI assets and the organization's risk appetite, preventing both under-protection of critical models and over-investment in low-impact controls.

## Scope

Applies to Kubernetes environments hosting AI workloads with significant business value or regulatory exposure. Covers asset identification, threat profiling, and risk prioritization for models, datasets, training pipelines, and inference services. Must be refreshed annually or upon major AI platform changes.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Enterprise risk management process, security architecture review cadence, and AI asset classification framework.
- Implementation Surface: Enterprise risk management process and OCTAVE three-phase assessment methodology, AI asset inventory with criticality classifications (models, training data, GPU infrastructure, inference endpoints), threat profile documentation per asset class, organizational risk tolerance and business impact analysis, security architecture review cadence, AI asset classification framework (proprietary vs. open models, PII vs. public datasets, production vs. staging endpoints), and risk treatment decision documentation with business justification.
- Owner Primary: Security Architecture
- Owner Supporting: AI/ML Engineering; GRC/Compliance; Business Stakeholders
