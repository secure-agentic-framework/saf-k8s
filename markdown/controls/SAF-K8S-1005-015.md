# SAF-K8S-1005-015 - Post-incident AI model integrity verification

- Domain: D10 - Observability, Incident Response, and Governance
- Knowledge Area: 10.5 - Incident Response for Kubernetes
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Organizations must verify AI model integrity after infrastructure compromises that could have affected AI workloads by performing signature, checksum, hash, or behavioral validation checks sufficient to determine whether restored or candidate models remain trustworthy.

## Objective

Ensure AI models are not silently compromised during infrastructure incidents by requiring post-incident integrity validation before restored or candidate models are returned to service.

## Rationale

Infrastructure compromises that touch AI workloads can introduce model tampering, backdoors, or corrupted artifacts that are not detectable through standard infrastructure recovery checks. Without dedicated post-incident model validation, organizations may restore and serve compromised models.

## Scope

Applies to AI workloads in Kubernetes clusters affected by a security incident. Covers post-incident model signature, hash, checksum, and behavioral validation workflows. Excludes the separate governance decision about whether to retrain, quarantine, or roll back based on those validation results.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Incident response recovery procedures and model integrity verification tooling.
- Implementation Surface: Model signature verification tooling, model registry checksum comparison, behavioral validation against reference datasets for statistical divergence detection, and post-compromise elevated monitoring used to confirm restored models remain trustworthy.
- Owner Primary: Security Operations
- Owner Supporting: AI/ML Engineering; Digital Forensics
