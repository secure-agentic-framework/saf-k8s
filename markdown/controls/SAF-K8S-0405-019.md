# SAF-K8S-0405-019 - Break-glass recovery procedure definition

- Domain: D04 - Identity, Access, and Secrets Management
- Knowledge Area: 4.5 - Identity Abuse Detection and Mitigation
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: baseline

## Statement

Organizations must define documented break-glass emergency access procedures that allow authorized personnel to regain cluster access when normal authentication pathways are unavailable.

## Objective

Ensure emergency recovery access paths are formally defined so authorized responders can restore administrative access during identity-provider, federation, or primary authentication outages.

## Rationale

Without a documented break-glass procedure, administrators may be unable to recover control-plane access during authentication failures, prolonging outages and increasing pressure for unsafe ad hoc workarounds.

## Scope

Applies to production Kubernetes clusters that depend on OIDC providers or external identity systems for normal administrative access. Covers documented runbooks, authorized activation steps, storage and retrieval workflow, and periodic validation exercises.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Break-glass runbook definition and controlled credential activation workflow.
- Implementation Surface: Documented break-glass runbooks, approved activation steps, emergency kubeconfig or client certificate retrieval procedures, and secure storage locations for recovery materials.
- Owner Primary: Security Engineering
- Owner Supporting: Platform Engineering; Identity and Access Management
