# SAF-K8S-1001-030 - Centralized AI workload log collection and routing

- Domain: D10 - Observability, Incident Response, and Governance
- Knowledge Area: 10.1 - Logging and Audit
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Organizations must collect and route AI workload logs, including training job logs, inference service operational logs, and pipeline execution logs, into the enterprise logging platform so investigators and operators have centralized visibility into AI workload activity.

## Objective

Provide unified visibility into AI workload activity by collecting AI-specific operational log streams into the enterprise logging platform.

## Rationale

AI workloads emit operational logs that are not fully captured by standard cluster logging. Without centralized collection and routing, investigators cannot reconstruct training failures, inference anomalies, or pipeline manipulation events across AI workloads.

## Scope

Applies to all AI/ML workloads running on Kubernetes, including training jobs, inference services, and ML pipeline controllers. Covers centralized collection and routing of AI workload logs into the enterprise logging platform. Excludes context-label segregation requirements and content-redaction logic applied before storage.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Log pipeline routing rules and centralized logging platform ingestion configuration.
- Implementation Surface: Log pipeline collection rules for training, inference, and pipeline controllers; cloud logging service routing for AI workload logs; and centralized logging platform indexes or views that ingest AI workload log streams.
- Owner Primary: Security Operations
- Owner Supporting: AI/ML Engineering; Data Privacy
