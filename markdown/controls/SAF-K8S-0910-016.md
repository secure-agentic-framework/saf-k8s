# SAF-K8S-0910-016 - Cross-cluster orchestration identity federation and authorization

- Domain: D09 - AI Workload Security: Training, Serving, and Pipelines
- Knowledge Area: 9.10 - Multi-Cluster and Federated AI Security
- Maturity: Advanced
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Multi-cluster AI workload orchestration control planes must authenticate cross-cluster API calls using short-lived federated workload identities and enforce authorization policies that restrict which orchestration principals may place, route, or schedule workloads across member clusters.

## Objective

Ensure that only trusted orchestration components can issue authorized cross-cluster workload actions.

## Rationale

Cross-cluster orchestration expands the attack surface beyond a single control plane. If orchestration identities rely on replicated kubeconfigs, long-lived tokens, or weak authorization, compromise of one environment can be leveraged to direct workloads or invoke actions across the federation from attacker-controlled infrastructure.

## Scope

Applies to organizations operating AI workloads across multiple Kubernetes clusters, whether on-premises, multi-cloud, or hybrid. Covers federated workload identity for cross-cluster orchestration and authorization policies governing placement, routing, and scheduling actions. Excludes centralized orchestration audit log retention and review.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Cross-cluster orchestration identity federation and authorization policy enforcement.
- Implementation Surface: Multi-cluster orchestration control planes, federated workload identity mechanisms such as SPIFFE/SPIRE or cloud-native workload identity federation, short-lived orchestration credentials, authorization policies restricting orchestration principals, and certificate rotation or revocation workflows for federated orchestration identities.
- Owner Primary: Platform Engineering
- Owner Supporting: ML Platform Engineering; Security Engineering; SRE
