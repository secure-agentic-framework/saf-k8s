# SAF-K8S-0904-015 - Experiment tracking metadata access control and tenant visibility enforcement

- Domain: D09 - AI Workload Security: Training, Serving, and Pipelines
- Knowledge Area: 9.4 - AI Pipeline Orchestration and Experimentation Security
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Experiment tracking systems must enforce access controls on experiment metadata, hyperparameters, metrics, and run history through project-, run-, and tenant-scoped authorization that limits visibility and modification to approved principals.

## Objective

Protect the confidentiality and integrity of experiment tracking metadata by restricting who can view and modify experiment records.

## Rationale

Experiment metadata exposes proprietary model configurations, hyperparameters, evaluation results, and tuning decisions. Without fine-grained authorization and tenant-scoped visibility limits, users can harvest sensitive ML intellectual property or alter experiment records outside their approved scope.

## Scope

Applies to experiment tracking platforms such as MLflow and Weights & Biases deployed on Kubernetes or used by Kubernetes-based ML platforms. Covers metadata authorization, project or run-level RBAC, and tenant-scoped visibility controls. Excludes audit logging, which is addressed separately.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Experiment tracking server authorization policy and tenant-scoping configuration.
- Implementation Surface: Experiment tracking platform authentication and authorization settings, project and run-level RBAC, tenant-scoped metadata visibility controls, and authorization tests demonstrating that unauthorized principals cannot read or modify experiment records.
- Owner Primary: ML Platform Engineering
- Owner Supporting: Security Engineering; ML Engineering
