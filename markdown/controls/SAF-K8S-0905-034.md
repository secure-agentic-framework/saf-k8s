# SAF-K8S-0905-034 - Canary and A/B candidate version isolation and traffic-splitting integrity

- Domain: D09 - AI Workload Security: Training, Serving, and Pipelines
- Knowledge Area: 9.5 - AI Supply Chain and Model Lifecycle
- Maturity: Advanced
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Canary and A/B deployment infrastructure must isolate candidate and stable model versions and preserve traffic-splitting integrity so a compromised or faulty candidate cannot interfere with the stable production version or silently capture unintended production traffic.

## Objective

Ensure multi-version serving infrastructure keeps candidate and stable versions isolated and enforces approved traffic distribution during staged rollout.

## Rationale

Without version isolation, a compromised candidate model can affect the stable model through shared resources or runtime interference. Without traffic-splitting integrity, a rollout controller or routing layer can silently direct more traffic to a candidate than intended, expanding the blast radius of a bad release.

## Scope

Applies to all multi-version model serving on Kubernetes, including canary deployments, A/B testing, and blue-green deployments. Covers resource and runtime isolation between versions and integrity of traffic-splitting configuration. Excludes rollback triggers and recovery automation, which are addressed separately.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Canary or A/B rollout controller configuration and service-mesh or ingress routing policy.
- Implementation Surface: Canary and A/B rollout controllers, Istio VirtualService or Envoy route configuration for traffic splitting, resource isolation between candidate and stable model versions, and isolation settings such as separate GPU devices or MIG partitions where needed.
- Owner Primary: ML Platform Engineering
- Owner Supporting: DevOps Engineering; Security Engineering
