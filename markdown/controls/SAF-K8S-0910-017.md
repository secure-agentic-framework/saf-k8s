# SAF-K8S-0910-017 - Centralized audit logging for cross-cluster orchestration actions

- Domain: D09 - AI Workload Security: Training, Serving, and Pipelines
- Knowledge Area: 9.10 - Multi-Cluster and Federated AI Security
- Maturity: Advanced
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Multi-cluster AI workload orchestration control planes must centralize audit logs for cross-cluster placement, routing, and scheduling decisions so orchestration actions remain attributable and reviewable after compromise or failure of an individual member cluster.

## Objective

Preserve traceability of cross-cluster orchestration actions through centralized audit collection and review.

## Rationale

Logs stored only on the orchestrating cluster or a member cluster can be destroyed, altered, or made unavailable during compromise. Centralized audit logging preserves the evidence needed to reconstruct cross-cluster placement and routing decisions and to investigate orchestration abuse after an incident.

## Scope

Applies to organizations operating AI workloads across multiple Kubernetes clusters, whether on-premises, multi-cloud, or hybrid. Covers centralized audit log collection for cross-cluster placement, routing, and scheduling decisions. Excludes federated identity issuance and authorization enforcement for cross-cluster APIs.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Centralized collection of orchestration audit logs for cross-cluster actions.
- Implementation Surface: Centralized audit logging pipelines, orchestration audit events recording requesting principal identity and target-cluster action, and log collection or retention settings for cross-cluster placement, routing, and scheduling decisions.
- Owner Primary: Platform Engineering
- Owner Supporting: ML Platform Engineering; Security Engineering; SRE
