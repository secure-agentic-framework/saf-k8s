# SAF-K8S-0703-014 - EDoS spending guardrails and autoscaling limits for AI resources

- Domain: D07 - Storage, Multi-tenancy, and Resource Governance
- Knowledge Area: 7.3 - Resource Governance and Priority
- Maturity: Advanced
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Organizations must implement Economic Denial of Sustainability (EDoS) controls for cloud-hosted AI infrastructure, including enforced auto-scaling maximum limits, cloud spending guardrails, and automated responses that prevent runaway GPU and compute spend from malicious or misconfigured workloads.

## Objective

Prevent uncontrolled cloud spending and resource amplification from AI workload auto-scaling by enforcing explicit economic guardrails on GPU and cloud resource consumption.

## Rationale

Cloud-hosted AI systems can turn abusive or faulty workloads into rapid economic loss through autoscaling and GPU allocation. Explicit spend ceilings, scaling limits, and automated enforcement reduce the financial blast radius of denial-of-wallet and resource amplification attacks before they exhaust budgets or impair other workloads.

## Scope

Applies to cloud-hosted Kubernetes clusters with auto-scaling enabled for AI workloads. Covers cloud spending guardrails, auto-scaling limits, queue and quota reactions tied to spend controls, and enforcement workflows for GPU-backed AI infrastructure.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Cloud auto-scaling maximum limits and spending guardrails with automatic enforcement.
- Implementation Surface: Cloud provider spending alerts with automatic enforcement, scale-down or job-suspension triggers, namespace quota reductions tied to budget policy, autoscaling caps for GPU node groups, and runbooks for denial-of-wallet containment.
- Owner Primary: SRE
- Owner Supporting: Platform Engineering; Security Engineering; FinOps
