# SAF-K8S-0405-021 - Break-glass activation multi-party approval enforcement

- Domain: D04 - Identity, Access, and Secrets Management
- Knowledge Area: 4.5 - Identity Abuse Detection and Mitigation
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: baseline

## Statement

Organizations must ensure break-glass access activation requires MFA or equivalent dual-authorization so emergency privileges cannot be enabled by a single actor without strong approval controls.

## Objective

Constrain break-glass activation through strong multi-party approval so emergency access does not become a single-actor privileged bypass.

## Rationale

Emergency access is inherently high risk because it bypasses normal identity paths. Requiring MFA or equivalent dual-authorization at activation reduces the likelihood that one person can improperly enable powerful recovery access during an incident.

## Scope

Applies to all break-glass credentials, kubeconfigs, or certificates issued for production Kubernetes clusters, including managed and multi-tenant environments. Covers activation controls, MFA or equivalent dual-authorization, and tenant-scoped emergency credentials where applicable.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Break-glass activation approval controls.
- Implementation Surface: MFA or equivalent dual-authorization at emergency access activation, activation approval workflows, and emergency access authorization records.
- Owner Primary: Security Engineering
- Owner Supporting: Platform Engineering; Identity and Access Management
