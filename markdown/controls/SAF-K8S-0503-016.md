# SAF-K8S-0503-016 - Namespace-scoped DNS service discovery restriction

- Domain: D05 - Network Security and Communication
- Knowledge Area: 5.3 - Ingress, Egress, and DNS Hardening
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: baseline

## Statement

DNS-based service discovery must be limited to authorized namespaces and approved service scopes so workloads cannot enumerate or resolve services outside their intended boundary.

## Objective

Restrict service discovery scope so shared DNS cannot expose cross-namespace or cross-tenant service information beyond authorized boundaries.

## Rationale

Shared DNS becomes a lateral-movement channel if workloads can enumerate or resolve services outside their intended namespace scope. Separating service-discovery scoping from resolution-path enforcement makes boundary control independently auditable.

## Scope

Applies to DNS egress restrictions, CNI DNS-aware policies, namespace-scoped service discovery controls, and any external DNS proxy or enforcement layer used by production workloads.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Namespace-scoped service discovery controls.
- Implementation Surface: Namespace-scoped service discovery policy configuration, DNS service visibility controls, and verification that workloads cannot resolve unauthorized cross-namespace services.
- Owner Primary: Network Engineering
- Owner Supporting: Platform Engineering
