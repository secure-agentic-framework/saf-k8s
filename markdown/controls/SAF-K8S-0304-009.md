# SAF-K8S-0304-009 - Temporary checkpoint storage encryption, integrity, and access control

- Domain: D03 - Workload and Pod Security
- Knowledge Area: 3.4 - Secure Defaults and Resource Constraints
- Maturity: Foundational
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

AI training jobs that store temporary checkpoints outside tmpfs must encrypt that storage, restrict checkpoint read and write access to authorized training identities, and verify checkpoint integrity before checkpoint reuse, resume, or promotion to later pipeline stages.

## Objective

Protect temporary checkpoint data from tampering and unauthorized access during multi-stage AI training workflows.

## Rationale

Temporary checkpoints can contain sensitive model state and training data derivatives, and they influence future training outcomes. Without encryption, scoped access control, and integrity verification, checkpoints can be read, altered, or replayed to introduce model backdoors or leak sensitive information.

## Scope

Applies to temporary or intermediate checkpoint storage used during AI training workflows, whether stored on ephemeral local disks, attached temporary volumes, or short-lived persistent storage prior to promotion or deletion. Long-term persistent volume encryption remains governed by storage security controls.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Checkpoint storage configuration and integrity validation processes for temporary training checkpoints.
- Implementation Surface: Temporary checkpoint storage encryption settings, service account or workload identity-based access controls on checkpoint locations, checksum or signature verification before checkpoint resume, and operational controls governing checkpoint lifecycle and cleanup.
- Owner Primary: AI/ML Platform team
- Owner Supporting: Platform Engineering; Security Engineering
