# SAF-K8S-0702-018 - Namespace tenant boundary model and isolation limitation documentation

- Domain: D07 - Storage, Multi-tenancy, and Resource Governance
- Knowledge Area: 7.2 - Namespace Isolation and Multi-Tenancy
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: baseline

## Statement

Organizations must define and document the namespace-based tenant isolation model for multi-tenant clusters hosting AI workloads and must document the isolation properties and limitations of namespaces as a security boundary relative to the threat model.

## Objective

Establish clear tenant boundary design decisions for namespace-based multi-tenancy and ensure operators understand both the intended isolation properties and the limitations of namespaces as a security boundary.

## Rationale

Namespaces provide logical separation but not complete security isolation by themselves. Without a documented boundary model and explicit statement of namespace limitations, teams adopt inconsistent tenant layouts and overestimate the protection namespaces provide. This design and documentation obligation is distinct from implementing the supplementary controls that enforce the chosen isolation level.

## Scope

Applies to all multi-tenant Kubernetes clusters where multiple teams, projects, or workload types share infrastructure. Covers selection and documentation of the namespace isolation model and its stated security-boundary limitations for AI and non-AI workloads.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Namespace design standards and documented tenant boundary decisions.
- Implementation Surface: Namespace isolation model documentation, tenant boundary design records, per-team or per-workload namespace patterns, vCluster or HNC design decisions, and documented statements of what namespaces do and do not isolate.
- Owner Primary: Platform Engineering
- Owner Supporting: Security Engineering; ML Engineering
