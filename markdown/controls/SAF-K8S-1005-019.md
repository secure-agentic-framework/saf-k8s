# SAF-K8S-1005-019 - Vulnerability disclosure policy, intake channels, and triage SLAs for Kubernetes AI infrastructure

- Domain: D10 - Observability, Incident Response, and Governance
- Knowledge Area: 10.5 - Incident Response for Kubernetes
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: baseline

## Statement

Organizations must publish and maintain a vulnerability disclosure policy for their Kubernetes platform and AI infrastructure that defines intake channels for internal and external reporters and severity-based triage SLAs for reported vulnerabilities.

## Objective

Ensure vulnerabilities affecting Kubernetes platform and AI infrastructure components are received through defined channels and triaged within established timeframes rather than handled ad hoc.

## Rationale

Kubernetes and AI infrastructure rely on many externally sourced components and internal platform services where vulnerabilities may be reported by employees, researchers, customers, or upstream communities. Without a published policy, intake path, and triage SLA, vulnerability reports are delayed or lost and remediation starts too late.

## Scope

Kubernetes control plane, node components, cluster add-ons, AI/ML operators, and GPU infrastructure vulnerabilities affecting the platform layer.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: governance
- Implementation Surface: Published vulnerability disclosure policy, security intake channels, severity definitions, and triage SLA workflow for Kubernetes and AI infrastructure issues.
- Owner Primary: Security Engineering
- Owner Supporting: Platform Engineering; ML Platform Engineering; Site Reliability Engineering
