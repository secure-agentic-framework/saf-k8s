# SAF-K8S-0901-007 - AI operator privilege management

- Domain: D09 - AI Workload Security: Training, Serving, and Pipelines
- Knowledge Area: 9.1 - Distributed Training Workload Security
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

AI-specific Kubernetes operators (NVIDIA GPU Operator, KServe, Kubeflow, Ray, Volcano, Kueue) must be deployed with least-privilege RBAC scoping, namespace restrictions, dedicated service accounts, and maintained under a defined vulnerability management and upgrade cadence.

## Objective

Minimize the blast radius of AI operators that require elevated cluster privileges by constraining their RBAC scope, isolating their operational namespaces, and ensuring timely patching of operator vulnerabilities.

## Rationale

AI operators often require cluster-wide privileges to manage CRDs, schedule GPU workloads, and configure device plugins. These elevated privileges make operators high-value targets; a compromised operator can gain broad cluster access. Without RBAC scoping and namespace restrictions, operator compromise grants lateral movement across the cluster. Without vulnerability management, known CVEs in operators and their dependencies remain exploitable.

## Scope

Applies to all AI-specific operators deployed on Kubernetes clusters, including NVIDIA GPU Operator, KServe, Kubeflow, Ray, Volcano, and Kueue. Covers operator service accounts, ClusterRole and Role bindings, namespace restrictions, and operator container images and dependencies. Excludes general-purpose Kubernetes operators not specific to AI workloads.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Operator deployment manifests, RBAC ClusterRole and Role bindings, operator namespace policies, and container image vulnerability scanning pipelines.
- Implementation Surface: AI operator deployment manifests (NVIDIA GPU Operator, KServe, Kubeflow, Ray, Volcano, Kueue), RBAC ClusterRole and Role bindings with least-privilege scoping, dedicated operator service accounts, operator namespace restriction policies, CRD access controls (InferenceService, RayCluster, PyTorchJob), NetworkPolicy for operator namespaces, container image vulnerability scanning pipelines, GitOps-pinned operator versions, and documented upgrade cadence and CVE tracking.
- Owner Primary: ML Platform Engineering
- Owner Supporting: Platform Engineering; Security Engineering
