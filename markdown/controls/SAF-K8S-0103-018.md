# SAF-K8S-0103-018 - Control-plane replica distribution and etcd quorum topology

- Domain: D01 - Control Plane and Cluster Hardening
- Knowledge Area: 1.3 - Controller-Manager, Scheduler, and Cloud Controller Security
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: baseline

## Statement

Production Kubernetes clusters must deploy control-plane components in a highly available configuration with a minimum of three replicas distributed across distinct failure domains and must configure etcd with an odd number of members sufficient to preserve quorum during a component or failure-domain outage.

## Objective

Maintain control-plane resilience through replica placement and quorum-safe etcd topology that survives individual node or failure-domain loss.

## Rationale

Leader election does not preserve availability when control-plane replicas or etcd members are concentrated in a single failure domain or sized without quorum tolerance. Requiring distributed replicas and quorum-safe etcd topology ensures the control plane can continue operating when nodes, racks, or zones fail.

## Scope

Applies to production clusters. Covers control-plane replica count and placement and etcd member topology. For managed Kubernetes, verify provider HA replica and quorum guarantees.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Control-plane topology and etcd quorum architecture
- Implementation Surface: Control-plane topology inventory, replica placement across failure domains, etcd member configuration, and availability architecture documentation.
- Owner Primary: Platform Engineering
- Owner Supporting: Site Reliability Engineering; Security Engineering
