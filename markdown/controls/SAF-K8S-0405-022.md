# SAF-K8S-0405-022 - Tenant-scoped break-glass credential boundary enforcement

- Domain: D04 - Identity, Access, and Secrets Management
- Knowledge Area: 4.5 - Identity Abuse Detection and Mitigation
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: baseline

## Statement

Organizations must ensure break-glass credentials, kubeconfigs, or certificates are tenant-scoped where applicable so emergency access is constrained to the minimum necessary tenant or operational boundary.

## Objective

Limit break-glass credential scope so emergency access cannot cross tenant or broader operational boundaries beyond the affected environment.

## Rationale

Break-glass access should restore control without granting unnecessary cross-tenant reach. Tenant-scoped emergency credentials reduce blast radius and prevent a recovery path in one environment from becoming a broad administrative bypass in another.

## Scope

Applies to all break-glass credentials, kubeconfigs, or certificates issued for production Kubernetes clusters, including managed and multi-tenant environments. Covers activation controls, MFA or equivalent dual-authorization, and tenant-scoped emergency credentials where applicable.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Break-glass credential scope boundary enforcement.
- Implementation Surface: Tenant-scoped break-glass credentials or kubeconfigs, scoped issuance workflows, and emergency credential configuration showing boundary-limited access.
- Owner Primary: Security Engineering
- Owner Supporting: Platform Engineering; Identity and Access Management
