# SAF-K8S-0402-002 - Inactive service account and stale credential remediation

- Domain: D04 - Identity, Access, and Secrets Management
- Knowledge Area: 4.2 - Service Accounts and Workload Identity
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: baseline

## Statement

Organizations must implement processes to identify inactive service accounts that have not authenticated within a defined period, disable or remove them, and revoke any stale credentials including legacy static tokens associated with those accounts.

## Objective

Reduce the attack surface by eliminating dormant service accounts and their associated credentials, which represent unmonitored pathways for unauthorized cluster access.

## Rationale

Service accounts created for decommissioned workloads, past experiments, or deprecated operators often retain their RBAC bindings and tokens indefinitely. Attackers who discover these stale credentials gain access to the cluster without triggering alerts associated with active workloads. Legacy static service account tokens, unlike projected tokens, never expire and remain valid until explicitly deleted.

## Scope

Applies to all service accounts across all namespaces in production and pre-production clusters. Covers both Kubernetes-native service account tokens and any external credentials (cloud IAM roles, Vault tokens) bound to those service accounts. Automated cleanup must respect system service accounts that may authenticate infrequently.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Service account lifecycle management tooling, scheduled audits of service account last-used timestamps, and automated workflows for disabling or deleting inactive accounts and revoking associated tokens.
- Implementation Surface: Kubernetes API server audit logs (service account last-used timestamps), service account lifecycle management tooling, scheduled cluster scans for inactive accounts, legacy static token Secret scanning (kubernetes.io/service-account-token type), automated workflows for disabling or deleting inactive accounts, and external credential revocation (cloud IAM roles, Vault tokens) bound to stale service accounts.
- Owner Primary: Identity and Access Management
- Owner Supporting: Platform Engineering; Security Engineering
