# SAF-K8S-0104-017 - CIS Benchmark exception approval and re-review governance

- Domain: D01 - Control Plane and Cluster Hardening
- Knowledge Area: 1.4 - CIS Benchmarks and Patch Management
- Maturity: Foundational
- Classification: baseline

## Statement

Any accepted exceptions to failing CIS Kubernetes Benchmark checks must be formally approved by a risk owner, documented with justification and compensating controls, and re-reviewed on a defined schedule so that waived benchmark findings remain explicitly governed risk decisions.

## Objective

Ensure waived benchmark findings are governed as explicit risk acceptances with approval, justification, compensating controls, and scheduled re-review.

## Rationale

Security teams often accumulate informal waivers that are never re-examined. This control isolates exception governance so organizations can independently prove that accepted benchmark gaps remain documented, approved, and periodically revalidated.

## Scope

Applies to all production clusters and to any CIS Benchmark findings accepted as exceptions rather than remediated immediately.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Risk Acceptance Governance
- Implementation Surface: GRC exception approval workflow, risk-owner attestations, compensating control records, and scheduled re-review process for benchmark exceptions.
- Owner Primary: Security Engineering
- Owner Supporting: Platform Engineering; Governance Risk and Compliance
