# SAF-K8S-0910-032 - Cross-cluster traffic anomaly monitoring for distributed AI workloads

- Domain: D09 - AI Workload Security: Training, Serving, and Pipelines
- Knowledge Area: 9.10 - Multi-Cluster and Federated AI Security
- Maturity: Advanced
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Cross-cluster network communication for distributed AI workloads must monitor traffic for anomalous sources, destinations, volumes, or transfer patterns that may indicate misuse, lateral movement, or data exfiltration across cluster boundaries.

## Objective

Detect suspicious cross-cluster AI communication patterns that authorized peer connectivity alone may not prevent or explain.

## Rationale

Even when cross-cluster peers are authenticated and authorized, allowed communication paths can still be abused for misuse, lateral movement, or covert transfer activity. A dedicated monitoring control is needed to surface suspicious cross-cluster traffic patterns early enough for investigation before material harm occurs.

## Scope

Applies to all cross-cluster network communication for distributed AI workloads, including distributed training gradient exchange, multi-cluster inference routing, and cross-cluster pipeline data transfer. Covers telemetry collection, dashboarding, and anomaly detection for inter-cluster communication patterns.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Cross-cluster traffic telemetry collection, dashboarding, and anomaly detection.
- Implementation Surface: Cross-cluster traffic dashboards, anomaly detection rules for unexpected sources and data volumes, and alert generation for suspicious inter-cluster communication patterns.
- Owner Primary: Network Engineering
- Owner Supporting: Platform Engineering; Security Engineering; ML Platform Engineering
