# SAF-K8S-0405-020 - Break-glass recovery exercise validation

- Domain: D04 - Identity, Access, and Secrets Management
- Knowledge Area: 4.5 - Identity Abuse Detection and Mitigation
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: baseline

## Statement

Organizations must periodically test break-glass emergency access procedures to verify authorized personnel can successfully regain cluster access when normal authentication pathways are unavailable.

## Objective

Verify through recurring exercises that defined emergency recovery access paths function as intended under realistic outage conditions.

## Rationale

An untested break-glass path may fail during an actual outage because credentials, storage, approvals, or recovery steps are stale or incomplete. Periodic validation reduces recovery risk and exposes procedural drift before an incident.

## Scope

Applies to production Kubernetes clusters that depend on OIDC providers or external identity systems for normal administrative access. Covers documented runbooks, authorized activation steps, storage and retrieval workflow, and periodic validation exercises.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Break-glass exercise execution and recovery validation.
- Implementation Surface: Scheduled break-glass exercises, recovery test execution records, after-action reviews, and validation of emergency kubeconfig or client certificate usability during simulated authentication outages.
- Owner Primary: Security Engineering
- Owner Supporting: Platform Engineering; Identity and Access Management
